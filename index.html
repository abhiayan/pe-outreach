Sun Auto entry strategy — critical

5 active Sun Auto contacts (Ripani, Pazderny, Powell, Loa, Castle). Treat the platform as one shot, not five. They compare notes in Tuesday ops meetings.

  1. Day 1. Send Chris Ripani. His Modern Tire Dealer quote is the hook. Highest-leverage send on the entire list.
  2. Day 3. Only if Ripani hasn't replied in 48 hrs, send Chris Pazderny. Same problem framing. Don't mention Ripani.
  3. Week 2+. If either Chris engages, ask which Regional VP to coordinate with. Don't pre-message Powell or Loa.
  4. Never (this round). Don't message Shawn Castle. Wrong function (CX, not ops).
  5. Hold. Tony Puckett (CEO) — only after Ripani engages.
Mavis entry strategy — critical

5 active Mavis contacts (Sisson, Fetters, Rush, Smith, Guardino). Same single-entry rule. Mavis closed Midas in June 2025 — now 3,500+ stores including 1,300 Midas franchises. The integration variance is THE strategic problem at HQ right now.

  1. Day 1. Send Brian Sisson with the Midas integration hook. SVP Retail Operations across the full network.
  2. Day 3. If silent, send Tracy Fetters. Express Oil heritage = strongest mechanical fit.
  3. Week 2+. Regional VPs (Rush GA, Smith FL, Guardino PA) only after an exec opens the door.
  4. Skip. Trey Cook (M&A, wrong function — but note as possible warm bridge to Randy Harris) and Richard Fackrell (store construction, wrong function).

Fit caveat. Pure Mavis stores are a shallower fit than Sun Auto. The VVL solenoid case study lands strongest with Express Oil-heritage folks (Fetters) or Midas integration framing (Sisson). For pure tire/brake regional VPs, lean on brake/alignment diagnostic variance instead.

Daily rhythm
  1. 20-minute calendar block. Same time daily. Campaign dies without rhythm.
  2. 5–10 sends per day max. Quality over volume. LinkedIn throttles spray-and-pray.
  3. Warm before you reach out. Like + thoughtful comment on a recent post 1–24 hours before sending the invite. Don't pitch in the comment.
  4. One follow-up only. Day 7 if no reply. Tone: "Bumping this — even a 'not now' is helpful." Then drop it.
When someone replies
  1. Reply within 4 hours. Same day minimum. Lag kills momentum.
  2. One goal in the reply: book the 20-min call. Not pitch. Not "tell me more."
  3. Attach the case study PDF here — this is the right moment, not in the opener.
  4. Suggest 3 specific times. "Tue 2pm / Wed 10am / Thu 4pm — which works, or want me to propose more?"
Questions for the first call
  1. Across your shops, where's variance widest — between top and bottom-quartile shops, or between A-techs and B-techs within the same shop?
  2. Of the complex diagnostic categories — drivability, electrical, ADAS, HVAC — which is the biggest senior-tech bottleneck right now?
  3. If you ran a pilot at one shop, what metric would have to move for it to be worth scaling?
  4. Who else in your org would need to weigh in?

Don't pitch on call #1 unless they ask. Listen, dump answers into the contact's Notes after the call.

Benchmarks to watch
  • Connection acceptance: 40–60% cold, 70%+ if warmed up first. Under 30% → profile or invite text isn't landing.
  • Reply rate: 15–25% on cold-but-targeted LinkedIn DM. Under 10% → opener is too generic.
  • Reply → call: 40–50%. Under that → the free pilot offer isn't being made clearly enough.
  • Call → pilot: 25–40%. Higher → not asking sharp enough qualifying questions.
  • Stop sequencing rule: If you're under 10% reply rate after 25 sends, stop and rework the opener. Don't grind through the rest of the list with a broken message.
